Who can benefit?

1

Women

The eating habitats of women changes as they grow over time. In this busy world, people are more inclined to eat junk food than organics, such as whole grains, fruits, fat-free diaries, and lean protein. We help you create a balanced eating pattern and the right food according to your age, profession, and food styles. Women typically have fewer muscles, and are in the requirement of proper calories to maintain healthy body weight and activity level ratio.

2

Older Adults

As you grow older, your dietary choices will likely evolve. For one thing, you may notice changes in appetite and how you feel about food, especially during medical conditions. Dieticians understand adults and determine precisely what they should increase and limit their diets to improve their overall health, lower their risks for particular diseases and health conditions, and generally feel their best.

3

During Pregnancy

Following a healthy lifestyle before, during, and after pregnancy has lots of benefits for both you and your developing baby and is an excellent investment in your baby’s future health. A healthy diet ensures you have a good store of nutrients to meet the demands of your developing baby, and keeps you fit and well. Healthy eating during pregnancy may also protect your baby against disease in later life.

4

Patients

Dieticians in hospitals and nursing homes educate patients and family members on eating well. They work with them to make sure that diets meet individual needs. They develop menus to meet patients’ nutritional needs. For example, they adapt menus for people recovering from illness or surgery, or with health conditions.

5

Sport Persons

Dieticians help athletes, sporting clubs, children, and people interested in fitness to eat better. They do this to help them achieve their sporting and fitness goals. For example, they may advise on making peak sporting performance or improving energy and activity levels.
